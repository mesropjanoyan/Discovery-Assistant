Description

Evaluate something as a group in order to find out what you really love about it, what might not be working and how to improve it.

Before:

You'll need to üìã Affinity Mapping to help you with this tactic.

During:

After:

Afterwards, consider using üìå Impact Effort Mapping to prioritize the potential opportunities.

Instructions

  1. Select and agree on the thing you want to evaluate.
    For example: a design, a project, an idea, a service, your life choices, the movie you just saw.
  1. Write a short description or find a suitable image of the thing.
  1. Put it on the wall so everyone can see it clearly.
  1. Explain the sticky note color system:
    • Rose: red sticky notes (positive aspects of the thing)
    • Thorn: yellow sticky notes (negative aspects, or things to watch out for)
    • Bud: green sticky notes (potential opportunities to grow the idea)
  1. Using this system, ask your group to write as many points as they can within a time limit.
  1. üìã Affinity Mapping the sticky notes and take it in turns to discuss each person's point.
