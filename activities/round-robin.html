About Evolve each other's ideas quickly. For example, when you already have a rough outline of an idea, but you're ready for it to evolve into something more robust.
Type

Description

You already have a rough outline of an idea but you want to develop it further. Thinking of what the next "version" of the idea is will help evolve it into a more robust and fully-formed solution. Collaboration doesn't get more ruthlessly efficient than this.

Instructions

  1. Give each person a large sheet of paper. Ask them to fold their piece of paper so that it is divided into four equal sections.
  1. With the paper in portrait, ask the group to write the name of their idea and the problem it solves at the top.
  1. Give them 5 minutes to draw their idea in the top left box with enough detail that someone can understand it without explanation.
  1. Afterwards, instruct everyone to pass their paper to the person on their left.
  1. The next person should look at the previous idea and develop it further, drawing their version in the next section of the paper. Additionally, they can include a statement about why the previous idea would fail.
  1. Carry on doing this until all the sections of the paper are full.
  1. Put the final ideas into a üìã T-Bar Format and present them back to the group.
