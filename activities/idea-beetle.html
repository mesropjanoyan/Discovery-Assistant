Duration 30 mins
Session
About Evaluate if your idea is strong and has "legs", or if it needs more thinking.
Type

Description

You might think you have a really good idea, but by evaluating it against a set of questions, you can quickly test its robustness. Fun fact: One in every four animals on the planet is a beetle. Similarly, one in every four business meetings is a total waste of time.

Before:

If you don't have any ideas to evaluate, run üí° Wild Eights first. It can be helpful to prioritize your ideas with üìå Priority Mapping beforehand, so you can evaluate ideas with the most potential.

During:

After:

Instructions

  1. Draw the Idea Beetle on a large surface.
  1. In the head, write the name of the idea. In the body, describe the idea.
  1. For each leg, write the answer to either or both set of questions:
    1. Who is it for, and how does it help them?
    1. What makes it different to what already exists?
    1. What happens to the idea if you reverse it?
    1. How could you grow this idea?
    1. Why would people use this again and again?
    1. How would explain it clearly in one sentence?
    1. Is the concept of the idea simple?
    1. Is the idea unexpected?
    1. Is the idea concrete? Can you visualize it in your head?
    1. Is the idea credible, and is there enough detail?
    1. Is the idea emotional? What does it make people feel?
    1. Is there a story? How do you tell others about the idea?
